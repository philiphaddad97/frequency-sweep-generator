About

A free, browser-based frequency sweep generator

What it does

The Frequency Sweep Generator produces continuous audio tones that sweep from a starting frequency to an ending frequency over a chosen duration. It supports four waveforms — sine, square, sawtooth, and triangle — and two sweep curves: linear and exponential (logarithmic).

What it's useful for

  • Speaker and headphone testing — find rattles, resonances, and frequency dropouts.
  • Room acoustics — measure room modes and standing waves.
  • Hearing range checks — sweep through the audible spectrum to find your personal cutoff (this is not a medical test).
  • Audio production — generate reference tones and risers for music and sound design.
  • Education — demonstrate frequency, waveforms, and the perception of pitch.

How it works

All audio synthesis happens in your browser using the Web Audio API. There is no server-side rendering, no upload, no cloud processing. The sweep itself is driven by the browser's audio thread using OscillatorNode.frequency.exponentialRampToValueAtTime() (or linearRampToValueAtTime()), giving sample-accurate frequency automation.

Downloads are also rendered locally: the same parameters are run through an OfflineAudioContext to produce a buffer, which is then encoded to WAV (16-bit PCM) or MP3 (128 kbps mono, via lamejs).

Privacy

Your audio settings and generated files never leave your device. The Site uses Cloudflare for hosting and may display advertisements; see our Privacy Policy for details.

Tech stack

  • HTML, CSS, vanilla JavaScript — no frameworks, no build step.
  • Web Audio API for synthesis and offline rendering.
  • lamejs (MIT licensed) for MP3 encoding in the browser.
  • Static hosting on Cloudflare Pages.
